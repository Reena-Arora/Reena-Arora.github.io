    Internship Projects

    Preparation and photodynamic efficacy of gold nanoparticle- rose Bengal complex on colon cancer cells

    Mentor: Prof.Dr.Abha Uppal (Bhabha atomic research centre, RRCAT Indore) | May’15-Dec’15

    • studied about nanoparticles, chemical synthesis of gold nanoparticles, characterization: uv spectrophotometric techniques, TEM and SEM microscopy, Fluorimetry, ultrasonication, handling of cell culture, suspension and preservation of cell lines

    • Covalently & electrostatically Attached photosensitizer (rose Bengal) to the surface of cysteamine coated gold nanoparticles

    • Characterised synthesized bare and photosensitizer conjugated nanoparticle for size, zeta potential, and spectroscopic properties

    • Tested drug nanoparticles complexes for dark & light induced toxicity using cell lines

    • Noticed significant increase in toxicity when the drug is covalently attached to the nanoparticle surface as compared to free drug

    • Observed higher singlet oxygen yield as compared to free Rose Bengal due to higher intracellular uptake


    Poster Presentations - 25th National laser Symposium Bhubneshwar

    Evaluation of photodynamic efficacy of Rose-Bengal conjugated to Cysteamine capped gold nanoparticles | Dec'16

    • In this presentation, we mainly focussed on synthesis of stable conjugates of RB with Gold nanoparticles and their characterization for photodynamic efficacy in colon cancer cells.

    • Results of the study suggest that covalent conjugation of rose Bengal with GNP leads to higher uptake, ROS yield and enhanced stability. Because of these factors, the photodynamic efficacy is also considerably enhanced.
